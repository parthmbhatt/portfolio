What?

An interactive FPV (First-Person View) tank battle game on an 8ft x 8ft arena.

Why?

For my first-ever OpenSauce Exhibit, I wanted to create something that was:

  • Interactive
  • Immersive
  • Delightful
  • Both art and engineering

How?

Platform: 1:30 Scale Heng Long M26 Pershing tank toy
FPV: Walksnail FPV HD Cam + TX and Walksnail RX kit
Terrain: Pink Extruded Polystyrene

When?

March 17, 2025 - July 17, 2025 (17 weeks)

System

The main challenge here was to build a cohesive experience around an existing platform, while supporting very low latency Video streaming in an extremely noisy RF environment.
This involved reverse-engineering the RC tanks' electrical layout and signalling structure, then finding creative ways to interpret the internal system state.





Mechanical

The main challenge here was packaging the FPV hardware, cooling, battery, antennae, and UI elements into the existing tank geometry without compromising serviceability or the vehicleâ€™s silhouette. This involved redesigning the turret around the camera +TX  stack, preserving the original hull interface, and iterating through CAD and printed prototypes to balance fit, airflow, wiring access, and durability.

Electrical

The Turret system contains a 2S lipo pack that drives the TX unit + Camera, as well as a cooling fan. The TX system consumed far more power than I had initially budgeted for and this along with the need for active cooling (This TX unit is designed to be in a drone, not a land vehicle)

The hull system (Green) consists of The RC tank PCBA, which is connected to various I/Os (IR TX LED, IR RX LED, Life count LEDs, Motors, Machinegun LED), The custom Interface PCB (Which performs level-shifting, some switching, and exposes the Xiao's pins to the sensor tap wiring harness), and the 3.7V 1S Lipo pack (which drives both the RC Tank's native system, as well as the Xiao, and the 'UI' LEDs in the turret)
