Multi-Joint Electromechanical Interconnect Architecture

I owned the interconnect architecture for a humanoid platform, translating early program needs, component placement, motion constraints, and packaging constraints into a power and signal distribution strategy. The work involved mechanical packaging, cable management, connector selection, high-speed signaling constraints, vendor management over a highly compressed prototyoe schedule.

 
Role: Mehcanical design owner for interconnect architecture and prototype cable-system execution
 
System: Multi-joint electromechanical platform with distributed power, signal, and motion constraints
 
Partners: Product design, Electrical engineering, Industrial design, Systems engineering, various vendors (Actuators, conductor assemblies, CMs)
 
Methods: Architecture trade studies, cable sizing, connector selection, interface documentation, PCBA layout & design, vendor management

Problem

The platform needed reliable power and high-speed data distribution across multiple moving joints while still fitting within tight mechanical packaging. The architecture had to balance motion range, cable routing, connector space, serviceability, electrical constraints, reliability risk, and prototype build practicality, while keeping in mind the needs of future planned platforms.

My Role

I drove the interconnect architecture from early concept studies through prototype execution. My work included comparing cable-management strategies, defining power and signal routing requirements, coordinating connector and interface decisions, creating documentation for build teams, working with vendors, and supporting the physical integration of the cable system.

Approach

  • Created and maintained documentation of the physical interconnect system in order to aid with hitting our stringent packaging constraints 
  • Compared cable-management options against reliability, available volume, bend behavior, assembly access, signal integrity, and supplier feasibility.
  • Built spreadsheet-based subassembly sizing tool for our candidate dynamic cable management solution to support investigations from early morphology to detailed part and subassembly design. 
  • Coordinated interface definitions between mechanical and electrical stakeholders so connectors, PCBAs, harnesses, routing constraints, and assembly order stayed aligned.
  • Supported prototype execution by tracking cable definitions, documentation, fabrication needs, inventory, and delivery lead times.
